My Journey

Learning about programming is an ongoing process. This page tracks my journey as a programmer. Each milestone represents an important event full of growth, problem-solving, and a deeper understanding of computer science.

  • Where It All Began

    August 2020

    During the COVID-19 pandemic, a family neighbor gave me a book called Coding for Kids for Dummies by Camille McCue. At first, I was hesitant to read it, since I had never been particularly interested in computer programming before. But once I opened that book, the way I saw the world began to change. As I worked through the projects, I realized that code wasn't some impossible language of numbers, letters, and symbols. It was something I could actually create and understand to build whatever I could imagine. That moment marked the beginning of my journey into programming and my dream of one dayworking for Google.

  • High School Diploma

    August 2021 - May 2025

    My time in high school was focused on both academic growth and personal development. Throughout four years, I challenged myself by taking Honors and AP courses while also participating in Chaffey College's Dual Enrollment program. Chaffey College allowed me to take five college-level programming classes during my first 3 years of high school and receive a Certificate of Achievement in Programming Foundations. This gave me early exposure to college-level requirements. Alongside academics, I played competitive soccer, where I learned the value of discipline, teamwork, and perserverance. Balancing rigorous classes with athletics pushed me to become more organized, resilient, and motivated. These skills continue to shape how I approach learning, problem-solving, and long-term goals.

  • Chaffey College

    August 2021 - October 2023

    During high school, I pursued and received a Certificate of Achievement in Programming Foundations from Chaffey College, completing a series of progressively advanced courses that built a solid technical foundation. I began with CISPROG-1 and CISIWEB-72 in my freshman year, followed by CIS-1 and CISIWEB-74 in my sophomore year, and finished with CISPROG-5 in my junior year. These courses exposed me to both programming fundamentals and web development, allowing me to develop practical skills in programming, problem-solving, and project design. Earning this certificate demonstrated my ability to handle college-level coursework while still in high school and reinforced my commitment to pursuing a career in software development.

  • California Seal of Biliteracy - Spanish

    August 2021 - May 2025

    I earned the California Seal of Biliteracy by demonstrating advanced proficiency in both Spanish and English across reading, writing, listening, and speaking. This distinction reflects four years of formal Spanish study, including one year of AP Spanish, and my ability to communicate clearly and effectively in academic and real-world settings. It represents a sustained commitment to bilingual education, cultural understanding, and strong communication skills.

  • BYU Idaho

    September 2025 - April 2026

    Beginning in August 2025, I started taking courses at BYU–Idaho through BYU Pathway Worldwide to continue advancing my education and prepare for a hopeful transfer to BYU–Provo. During this time, I earned a Certificate in Web and Computer Programming, building on the foundation I established earlier in my journey. The program emphasized hands-on, project-based learning, where I developed dynamic web applications and strengthened my understanding of software design, object-oriented programming, and full-stack development. This experience not only expanded my technical skill set but also marked a transition from foundational knowledge to creating real-world applications.

  • BYU Provo

    April 2026 - Present

    I have now been accepted to and am currently continuing my education at Brigham Young University–Provo, where I am building on my computer science foundation through advanced coursework in programming, software development, and computational problem-solving. This experience is strengthening my ability to design efficient systems and deepening my technical expertise as I progress toward a career in intelligent systems.
